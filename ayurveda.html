What is Ayurveda?

Ayurveda, meaning "knowledge of life," is the world's oldest holistic healing system. Originating in India over 5,000 years ago, it's based on the belief that health and wellness depend on a delicate balance between mind, body, and spirit.

Unlike modern medicine that treats symptoms, Ayurveda focuses on identifying and addressing the root cause of imbalance. It recognizes that each person is unique, with their own constitution (Prakriti) and current state of balance (Vikriti).

Through personalized nutrition, lifestyle practices, herbal remedies, yoga, and meditation, Ayurveda guides you toward optimal health and spiritual growth.

The Three Doshas

Understanding your unique constitution is the foundation of Ayurvedic healing. Discover which dosha dominates your nature and learn how to maintain balance.

Air + Space

Vata

The energy of movement and change. Vata governs breathing, circulation, and nervous system function.

Characteristics:

  • • Creative and energetic
  • • Quick thinking
  • • Light sleeper
  • • Dry skin and hair

When Balanced:

Enthusiastic, flexible, and imaginative

When Imbalanced:

Anxious, restless, and scattered

Fire + Water

Pitta

The energy of transformation and metabolism. Pitta governs digestion, absorption, and body temperature.

Characteristics:

  • • Strong digestion
  • • Natural leader
  • • Medium build
  • • Warm body temperature

When Balanced:

Focused, confident, and courageous

When Imbalanced:

Irritable, aggressive, and impatient

Earth + Water

Kapha

The energy of structure and stability. Kapha governs immunity, growth, and physical strength.

Characteristics:

  • • Strong immunity
  • • Calm and steady
  • • Larger frame
  • • Thick hair and skin

When Balanced:

Loving, patient, and supportive

When Imbalanced:

Lethargic, possessive, and stubborn

Ayurvedic Yoga Practices

Integrate yoga with Ayurvedic principles to create a practice that balances your unique constitution and current state of being.

Vata Yoga

Grounding & Calming

Recommended Practices:

  • • Slow, grounding sequences
  • • Longer holds in poses
  • • Hip openers and forward folds
  • • Restorative yoga
  • • Deep breathing exercises

Best Times:

Morning and evening, consistent routine

Pitta Yoga

Cooling & Moderate

Recommended Practices:

  • • Moderate-paced flows
  • • Heart opening poses
  • • Twists and side bends
  • • Moon salutations
  • • Cooling pranayama

Best Times:

Early morning or evening, avoid midday heat

Kapha Yoga

Energizing & Dynamic

Recommended Practices:

  • • Dynamic, vigorous flows
  • • Sun salutations
  • • Standing poses and backbends
  • • Inversions
  • • Energizing breathwork

Best Times:

Morning practice, consistent daily routine

Seasonal Wisdom

Ayurveda teaches us to live in harmony with nature's rhythms. Discover how to adapt your practice and lifestyle with the changing seasons.

Spring

Kapha season - time for renewal and cleansing

Focus: Detoxification

Yoga: Dynamic flows

Diet: Light, warm foods

Herbs: Turmeric, ginger

Summer

Pitta season - time for cooling and moderation

Focus: Cooling practices

Yoga: Gentle, restorative

Diet: Fresh, cooling foods

Herbs: Mint, coriander

Autumn

Vata season - time for grounding and nourishment

Focus: Grounding practices

Yoga: Slow, steady flows

Diet: Warm, oily foods

Herbs: Ashwagandha, brahmi

Winter

Kapha season - time for building strength and immunity

Focus: Building strength

Yoga: Warming practices

Diet: Nourishing, warming

Herbs: Chyawanprash, ginger
